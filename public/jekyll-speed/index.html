A Hint on How to Speed Up Jekyll Regeneration

My Jekyll site was way too slow recently. It took around 1 minute per regeneration and much more for starting a server.

I made some improvement by excluding a few files from the conversion.

_config.yml: 

exclude: [file.js, somefolder, otherfolder, ...]

It wasn’t a big improvement so I kept digging. I re-installed gems. Didn’t help.

Then I found a solution that shortened the regeneration time to less than 0.2 second. It was excluding even more stuff.

Beside the blog, I have also projects that use different node modules and other JS. One ‘node_modules’ folder turned to be the pain point. I didn’t exclude it because it was hidden somewhere deeper in the directory.

I reorganized my files so now it’s easy to locate that kind of stuff. 

exclude: [file.js, somefolder, projects/*/node_modules, ...]
